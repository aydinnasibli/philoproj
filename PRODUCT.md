# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Curious generalists, students, and self-educators who want to explore the history of philosophy through visual connections rather than read academic text walls. They arrive in a state of discovery, not research. First-time visitors need a clear sense of what the project is before diving in; returning users are navigating a space that feels like their own.

## Product Purpose

The Living Manuscript is a living map of Western philosophical thought that traces the lineage, ideas, and connections of history's greatest thinkers. It merges data with visual appeal to make philosophy accessible and enjoyable. Success looks like a user spending time following connections they didn't expect to follow.

## Brand Personality

Warm, exploratory, elegant. The closest reference is Obsidian — a personal knowledge graph that feels like your own intellectual space — but more atmospheric, more editorial, and less utilitarian. Not a textbook. Not a wiki. A place where ideas feel alive and connected.

## Anti-references

- Wikipedia: clinical, plain, text-heavy, zero visual personality
- Generic SaaS dashboards: utility-first, cold, no sense of place
- Gamified learning platforms (Duolingo-style): playful to the point of trivializing the subject
- "Dark edgy philosophy" aesthetics: brooding, high-contrast neon, trying too hard

## Design Principles

1. **Connections over definitions** — the relationships between thinkers are the product; isolated facts are supporting material
2. **Warmth earns trust** — the parchment-and-gold aesthetic should feel like a well-loved book, not a UI template pulled from a library
3. **Visual first, text second** — the network graph and timeline are the primary interface; prose supports them, never competes
4. **Discovery over search** — users should stumble onto interesting ideas; the interface should reward wandering
5. **Restraint enables focus** — elegance means knowing when to step back so the ideas themselves can breathe

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific WCAG level mandated. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` for all animations. Aim for WCAG AA contrast ratios as a baseline for text legibility.
