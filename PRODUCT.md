# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Curious generalists browsing in discovery mode. They are not researching a specific philosopher or writing a paper; they arrive with loose interest and follow connections they didn't plan to follow. They need to understand what the product is within 10 seconds — clarity unlocks exploration, not the other way around.

## Product Purpose

The Living Manuscript is an interactive map of Western philosophical thought that traces the lineage, ideas, and connections between history's greatest thinkers. It is a tool for wandering with structure: five surfaces (Network graph, Lineage timeline, Schools, Thinkers archive, My Notes) that reward following threads rather than searching for answers. Success is a user who came to look up one philosopher and left having followed three unexpected connections.

## Brand Personality

Clear, confident, modern. The design makes strong decisions rather than hedging through atmosphere. It respects the subject without being stuffy. It is contemporary in sensibility without chasing visual trends.

## Anti-references

- **Wikipedia**: clinical neutrality, wall-to-wall text, zero visual personality, no sense of place. The product should feel nothing like this, even when showing dense information.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity earns exploration** — Users understand what the product is immediately. Discovery follows orientation; confusion kills it.
2. **Connections are the product** — The relationships between thinkers are the primary surface; biographical facts and definitions are supporting material.
3. **Strong decisions, not atmospheric hedging** — Make confident choices about type, space, and color. Restraint through precision, not through softening everything with texture or warmth.
4. **Respect the subject** — Philosophy is serious. The design should carry that weight without becoming academic or cold.
5. **Discovery by structure, not by accident** — Serendipity is designed in; it does not happen despite the interface.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No hard compliance target. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` for all animations. Aim for readable contrast ratios on all body text as a baseline.
